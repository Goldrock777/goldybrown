Personalisation that ages well

The mistake most wedding gifts make is freezing the piece to one weekend. An engraved date on the outside means he uses it on anniversaries. Personalised with his initials, he uses it forever.

What we recommend:

  • Hot-stamped initials on the outside — two letters (first and last), not three. Subtle, classic, tied to the man rather than the wedding.
  • Hand-tattooed designs for the bigger gifts — family crest, signature, sketch, monogram. Etched freehand into the front face, finished flush with the surface.
  • Wedding date on the inside if you must include it — under a flap, on the lining, where the recipient will find it years later.

What we'd skip: full-name engravings, hashtags, "best man" or "groomsman" titles, inside jokes. None age past year two.

Presentation

For wedding gifting in particular, the presentation does at least as much work as the leather. The piece arrives wrapped in unbleached cotton, sealed with the property's wax seal, with a handwritten card. It is the piece they unwrap at the rehearsal dinner that is photographed.

The wax seal and the card are the cheapest line items in the gift and the highest-leverage. Both are included on every order over $200 per piece.
