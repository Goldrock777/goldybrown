# Directory Submissions — Goldy Brown

Implements the `directory-submissions` skill from the marketingskills repo. The point is not just SEO backlinks — it's discoverability in the channels where buyers (and procurement teams) are already looking.

## How to use this list

Submit in batches of 5-10 per week. Track each in a spreadsheet:
| Directory | URL submitted | Date | Status | Notes |

Most directories: free. Some charge a one-time or annual listing fee — flagged below. Skip the SEO-only "submit your URL to 500 directories" services; Google explicitly devalues those backlinks.

## TIER 7 — pay-to-list (skip unless ROI is obvious)

Most are weak. Two exceptions worth considering:

- **Forbes Travel Guide preferred supplier** ($) — good for the Hotel Programme. Costs ~$5K/year; pays back if you close a single 5-star property.
- **Robb Report Curated** ($) — premium audience. Cost varies. Worth testing once the brand has a year of editorial press.

**Don't pay for**: "submit to 500 directories" services, generic SEO directories, link farms, sponsored business listing aggregators. Google penalises low-quality directory backlinks.

## Submission checklist (per directory)

Before each submission, prepare:
- Brand name, founded date, location, contact email and phone
- 50-, 150-, and 500-character descriptions (the 500-char one is on `/about/`)
- Logo (gb-silver.png), hero image (holster.png)
- Categories — typically "leather goods manufacturer" / "luxury accessories" / "B2B hospitality supplier"
- Hours, attributes, payment methods (mirrors GBP setup)
- Press kit URL: https://goldybrown.com/press/

## Cadence

- Week 1: Tier 1 (8 listings)
- Week 2-3: Tier 4 (B2B procurement portals — these take 30+ minutes each)
- Week 4: Tier 2 + 3 (10 listings)
- Week 5+: Niche, regional, and rolling outreach as opportunities surface

Track results in 3 and 6 months: which directories drove referral traffic? Double down on those, drop the rest.
