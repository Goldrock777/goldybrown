# Content Calendar — Q3 / Q4 2026

Implements the `content-strategy` skill. A 12-week editorial calendar that compounds the SEO work already done.

## The shape of the calendar

Three streams running in parallel:
1. **Journal articles** (1 per week) — long-form, rank for evergreen long-tail. The flywheel.
2. **GBP / social posts** (2 per week) — Instagram + Google Business Profile. Already-warm audience.
3. **Email broadcasts** (1 every 2 weeks) — list nurture once the newsletter capture goes live.

Match cadence to the team you actually have. If you're solo, drop to 1 journal article every 2 weeks and 1 social/week. Half is better than zero.

## Working rules

- **Length**: journal pieces aim for 700-1100 words. Long enough to be useful, short enough to read on a flight.
- **Voice**: quiet luxury, no name-drops, defensible language.
- **Internal linking**: each new piece links to 2-3 existing pages.
- **Imagery**: every journal piece needs at least one good photo. Reuse existing assets before commissioning new ones.
- **Cadence over polish**: a published 700-word draft beats an unpublished 1500-word draft. Ship.

## What the calendar replaces

If you're not running this calendar today, you're probably:
- Posting on Instagram once every 2-3 weeks when something feels "ready"
- Writing the occasional journal piece when inspiration hits
- Sending an email blast once per quarter

The calendar replaces "when inspiration hits" with "every Tuesday at 10am". That's the whole game.
