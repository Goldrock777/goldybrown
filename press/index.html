Founder & brand voice

Goldy Brown was founded by an entrepreneur who grew up between Vancouver and the South Indian leather corridor and saw the gap between "Made in France" labels and the actual origin of the leather inside many of the bags being sold. The brand is built on the conviction that the best leather goods are made in fewer steps with fewer middlemen, that lifetime warranties should not require a receipt, and that quiet luxury is more interesting than loud branding.

Coverage angles editors have found compelling: the body-worn travel category as an answer to airline carry-on enforcement, the Hotel Programme as a counter to generic hotel guest gifting, the South Indian tannery story, and the lifetime warranty as a counter to fast fashion's leather equivalent.

Pre-written boilerplate (paste into copy)

Goldy Brown is a Vancouver luxury leather atelier producing full grain calfskin travel bags and accessories — designed in Vancouver, made in South India by master artisans whose tannery houses have supplied European luxury for over a century. The brand's signature piece, the Holster, is a body-worn travel bag worn rather than carried. Goldy Brown also operates a Hotel Programme creating bespoke VIP guest amenity kits for 4- and 5-star hotels worldwide.
