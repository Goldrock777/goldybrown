The category, reconsidered

The travel bag market has been settled for thirty years: a wheeled carry-on for the overhead bin, a backpack or tote for the personal item under the seat. We don't sell either of those. We sell a third category — the body-worn travel bag — that solves the part of travel a backpack and a roller don't: keeping your passport, phone, and wallet on your body through every checkpoint, every flight, and every taxi line, without occupying a hand or counting as a third bag.

Body-worn travel bags are not a new idea. Cabin crew, journalists, and frequent flyers have used them for decades — fanny packs, money belts, slings, and chest rigs. What's new is the leather we make them in, the proportions we cut, and the tannery lineage they carry.

Why a body-worn travel bag

The most useful question to ask before buying a travel bag is: where does it go when you sit down on the plane? A backpack goes in the overhead bin or, if you booked the front of the cabin, under the seat in front. Either way, it leaves your body for the next eight hours, and your passport leaves with it.

A body-worn travel bag stays on you. It rides through security on you, through the gate on you, into the cabin on you, and against your collarbone for the redeye. When the seat-back ahead of you is in your face on a 9 a.m. arrival into LHR, your passport is exactly where it has been since you left home — at your chest, under your jacket, ready to hand to the gate agent without unzipping anything.

The test of a travel bag isn't capacity. It's whether the things you actually need are on your body or in a bin three rows away.

What "travelling compartment" actually means

A travelling compartment, in the leather goods vocabulary, is a dedicated section of a bag that holds the documents and small electronics a traveller uses at the airport — passport, boarding pass, phone, headphones — separated from the rest of the bag's contents. Better travel bags think about the compartment first; lesser ones treat it as an afterthought.

The Goldy Brown Holster is, in effect, just the travelling compartment — a piece sized exactly for what you reach for at security and on the plane, with everything else (clothes, books, laptop) left to a roller or a backpack. It's a deliberate scoping decision: do one thing, do it right.
