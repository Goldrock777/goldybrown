A TSA-Friendly Leather Travel Bag

A body-worn full grain leather travel bag built for the modern security checkpoint. Stainless steel hardware that doesn't flag scanners, a single YKK zipper, and proportions sized for the four things you reach for at the lane — passport, phone, wallet, AirPods. Lifetime warranty. Free worldwide shipping.

An honest note on "TSA-approved" TSA does not formally approve specific consumer travel bags. Any brand using "TSA-approved" on their packaging is, at best, stretching the term. We use TSA-friendly to mean: a bag that moves through TSA, CATSA, and international airport security without setting off scanners or creating friction. The Holster has done that across hundreds of personal flights through JFK, LAX, SFO, ORD, YVR, YYZ, LHR, CDG, and DXB. The on-the-ground report is that the bag is uneventful at the lane.

Why the Holster works at TSA

1. The hardware is stainless steel

The buckles, rivets, and zipper hardware are stainless steel — the same material as the buckle on your belt, the case of your watch, and the zipper on your jeans, all of which pass through metal detectors and body scanners every day without issue. Detectors are calibrated to the ambient mass of stainless that millions of travellers carry. They are not calibrated to a few stainless rivets in a leather strap.

The hardware to avoid in a travel bag is dense brass and iron-content metal — both of which are commonly found in cheaper goods and both of which trip detectors. We don't use either.

2. The proportions match TSA's expectations

The Holster pouch is 7" × 5". Inside it: passport, an iPhone 17 Pro Max with case, AirPods, a slim wallet, sunglasses. Nothing surprising on the X-ray. Officers scan thousands of bags a day and pull aside the ones that read as unusual. A leather pouch with predictable contents reads as a wallet, not a question.

3. It comes off in seconds

The Holster removes from the body in one motion — a single side-release buckle. Officers prefer items off and in a tray before you reach the scanner. Last-minute removal slows the lane. The Holster's design lets you remove it at the bag-table where shoes go, lay it flat in its own tray, walk through clean, and put it back on at the recovery bench. The whole interaction takes less time than threading a belt back through your loops.

What about the worn-accessory question?

The TSA-friendly question and the airline-carry-on question are different. TSA cares about scanning, not bag count. Airlines care about bag count, not scanning. The Holster is friendly to both: it scans cleanly because it has stainless hardware and predictable contents, and it doesn't count as a third bag because it's worn on the body — the same category as a money belt or fanny pack — rather than carried.

Frequently asked

Is the Goldy Brown Holster TSA-approved?

No — and we are clear about this. TSA does not formally approve specific consumer travel bags. We use the term TSA-friendly to mean a bag that moves through TSA, CATSA, and international security lanes without setting off scanners or creating friction. Practical experience has been uneventful across hundreds of personal flights.

Will the metal hardware set off the body scanner?

No. The fittings are stainless steel — the same material as belt buckles, watch cases, and zippers on jeans, all of which pass through scanners daily. The hardware to avoid is large brass or iron-content metal, not stainless.

Should I take the Holster off at the lane?

Yes. Take it off before you reach the scanner and place it in its own tray. Walk through. Collect it on the other side. Most disruption comes from travellers trying to walk through still wearing the bag.

Does the Holster count as a third bag?

It's worn on the body — same category as a money belt, fanny pack, or sling — and gate agents typically do not count it against carry-on or personal item allowances. Airline policy ultimately rests with the agent at the gate.

Is it allowed in TSA Pre-Check, NEXUS, and Global Entry lanes?

Yes. Trusted-traveller lanes are typically metal-detector lanes (no body scanner unless randomly selected), shoes-on, laptop-and-liquids-in-bag. The Holster usually goes off the body and through the X-ray separately.

What if I'm offered a pat-down?

Stay still, hands at your sides, let officers do their job. If you have any unusual personal medical items (CGM, port, stoma bag) or metal implants (knee, shoulder, stent), say so up front. The pat-down is over in 30 seconds. The detail is in our guide to airport security pat-down etiquette.
